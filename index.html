About

I am currently an Associate Professor in the Maersk Mc-Kinney Moller Institute at University of Southern Denmark as part of the Game Development and Learning Technologies group.

I have completed my PhD in Affective Music Generation and its Effect on Player Experience as part of the Center for Computer Games Research games at the IT University of Copenhagen.

My PhD research investigated the expression of moods and the affective effect this mood-expressive music can have on the listener and applied this research to games: the final objective was to create a system where, by using a cognitive model of the player, we would be able to identify the player's emotional state and be able to reinforce or manipulate it through the use of mood-expressive music to improve user experience.

What this research hopes to achieve is creating better immersive experiences (reinforcement of current emotional state and playstyle) and help the designers create experiences where the players are put in a specific emotional state (manipulation).

I completed my Master degree in Information Technology through the Media Technology and Games program of IT University of Copenhagen in June 2014. During my master studies I have developed a great interest in Procedural Content Generation for games, especially of music. My research at the time created the basis of what now is my PhD work: the expression of affective meaning through computer-generated music.

I have also developed different types of games (both large and small) during my education and in my spare time. I received my Bachelor degree in Computer Science from the Universit√† Statale di Milano in 2011, in which I specialized in artificial intelligence and mathematical logic.

I am currently teaching game development and continuing research on procedural content generation with, of course, a focus on music generation.

Mood Expressive Music Generator

This generator has been developed during part of my Master and the start of my PhD, it has been realized using PD (aka Pure Data), a real-time graphical programming environment for audio, video, and graphical processing. The generated music is played by 3 instruments (synthesizers) and a drum machine. The system consists of five random number generators: four of these numbers will be converted into notes for the instruments and the drums, whilethe last one is used as a sound effect controller.

It creates randomic music that can express different moods by manipulating a relatively small set of variables that are indipendent from harmony and melody. The resulting music sounds very ambient.

Currently I'm developing a new generator that will probably not use PD and hopefully will be able to express a bigger musical space and produce more interesting music.

SMUG: Scientific Music Generator

Music is based on the real world. Composers use their day-to-day lives as inspiration to create rhythm and lyrics. Procedural music generators are capable of creating good quality pieces, and while some already use the world as inspiration, there is still much to be explored in this. We have developed a system to generate lyrics and melodies from real-world data, in particular from academic papers. Through this we wanted to create a playful experience and establish a novel way of generating content (textual and musical) that could be applied to other domains, in particular to games. For melody generation, we have used a novel approach to Markov chains evolution.

Our system takes in input a PDF of an academic paper and outputs lyrics and relative musical melody. Check out the online version of our system!

Four Part Harmony Co-Evolution

This project evolves four part harmonies, either with a given bass or free. The evolution uses the Multiple Worlds Model defined by Joseph Brown.

The model makes distinct populations evolve independently, but when calculating the fitness function requires the individuals from the various populations to work together. In our case we have a population for each of the four voices and the fitness function is calculated by considering the voices singing together.

This approach leads to speciation, where each of the voices evolve in best way to work together with the other ones.

Metacompose

Metacompose is an affective expressive real-time music generator that is designed to be used in videogames.

What does that mean? It means that it can be used to produce music in real-time, absolutely from scratch, that can be charged with some affective meaning (moods and maybe emotions).

What is the goal? Imagine that you're playing a game, you'd like the music to react and reflect actions that you do in game, right? But what about emotion? You'd like the game to reflect for example the mood of the game or the stressfullness of a situation. I believe music is nothing if we strip it away from its function as a communication tool, and in music we communicate through emotion. If we can express moods through music we can reflect how the player's emotional state is, reinforcing it, or try to manipulate it. The final goal is to provide the player with a better experience and the designer/developer with a tool that can be used to guide user experience through music.

How does it work? This image is from John Cage's book Notations, this book aimed at showing how conventional music notation is at best an approximation of music and is very limiting in the sense that the score is usually considered the "music".
As we want Metacompose to be able to change in real-time according to the player's actions we needed to have a more flexible representation: this is why we actually generate abstractions of music, that are then interpreted by some "players" to create music, as in a jam-session.
The expression of affective states is separate from the abstraction generation: the instruments change the way they play to match a desired mood. This is done accordingly to my mood expression theory that you can find described in my papers.

Interactive Story Generation

This is a project developed in the span of three days during the Prosecco CodeCamp 2016 held in Antwerp.

The core idea of our project was to create something more interactive than pure text generation. Given our shared interests, we quickly converged on the idea of creating a game-ish ‚Äúchoose your own adventure‚Äù text game. At first we wanted to be able to create a Hero‚Äôs Journey type of structure and use various archetypes to structure interactions between characters. To this end, we chose four archetypes (the Hero, the Mentor, the Trickster and the Shadow) and had to find a way to annotate the character of the NOC list with which archetypes they mostly reflect. We identified some of the main characteristics of these archetypes and programmatically annotated a list of characters with them.

While the system developed is very limited, it creates fun stories and interactions that are interesting to read. Better still, it is challenging as it forces the user to imagine ahead how the characters will react to actions they do, which is very seldom intuitive and banal.
